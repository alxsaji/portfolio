Context

This project emerged from a critical need for business intelligence in the online retail sector. The business was operating with vast amounts of transaction data but lacked systematic analysis to understand sales trends, customer behavior, and operational opportunities.

The challenge stems from the abundance of order data but lack of analytical capabilities to extract actionable insights. Without proper data analysis, the business couldn't make informed decisions about inventory management, marketing strategies, or operational improvements.

Database Overview

The analysis utilized a comprehensive relational database with 8 normalized tables containing retail order data:

  • ONLINE_CUSTOMER: Customer information including names, email, phone, gender, and creation dates
  • ORDER_HEADER: Order details including order dates, status, payment mode, payment dates, and shipment dates
  • ORDER_ITEMS: Order line items linking orders to products with quantities
  • PRODUCT: Product catalog with descriptions, prices, quantities available, dimensions, and weights
  • PRODUCT_CLASS: Product classification and categories
  • ADDRESS: Customer and shipper addresses with location details
  • SHIPPER: Shipping company information
  • CARTON: Packaging dimensions for shipping optimization

The database contained data from multiple countries including India, USA, Singapore, Malaysia, and Sri Lanka, providing a comprehensive view of international retail operations.

Problem

There is an abundance of order transaction data but lack of reliable analytical methods for business teams to determine sales trends, customer behavior patterns, and operational opportunities to support strategic decision-making.

How Might We

"How might we extract actionable insights from retail order data to understand sales trends, customer behavior, and inventory needs, enabling data-driven decisions for inventory management and marketing strategies?"

Business teams face several key challenges:

  • Difficulty identifying seasonal purchasing patterns and trends
  • Lack of understanding of customer lifetime value and behavior
  • Inability to identify high-value customer segments
  • Limited visibility into product performance metrics
  • Challenges in optimizing inventory levels and cash flow

Project Goal

Make the process of retail data analysis reliable, comprehensive, and actionable for the business, enabling them to make data-driven decisions about inventory management, marketing strategies, and operational improvements.

Solution

"Extract business insights with precision, depth, and actionability – the goal is to Enable Data-Driven Decision Making."

Developed a comprehensive SQL-based analysis framework that extracts actionable insights from retail order data. The solution provides deep visibility into sales trends, customer behavior, and operational metrics to support strategic business decisions.

Core Features

Sales Trend Analysis

Approach

The project was developed using a systematic SQL analytics methodology, following best practices in database design, query optimization, and business intelligence. The approach was divided into four key phases: Database Design, Query Development, Analysis & Insights, and Reporting Automation.

Phase 1

Database Design & Normalization

"How do we structure the database for efficient analysis?"

Designed and implemented a normalized relational database schema with 8 tables establishing proper relationships between orders, customers, products, addresses, shippers, and cartons. Created primary keys, foreign keys, and appropriate data types for each table. Ensured referential integrity through foreign key constraints. Designed the schema to support efficient querying and analysis of retail operations across multiple dimensions.

Phase 2

Query Development

"What queries do we need to extract key business insights?"

Developed comprehensive SQL queries using JOINs (INNER, LEFT, RIGHT), subqueries, window functions, aggregate functions, and Common Table Expressions (CTEs) to analyze sales trends, customer behavior, and product performance. Created queries for calculating key metrics including total sales, average order value, customer lifetime value, repeat purchase rates, product performance, order status distribution, payment method analysis, and geographic sales patterns. Used GROUP BY, HAVING, ORDER BY, and WHERE clauses to filter and aggregate data effectively.

Phase 3

Analysis & Insights

"What insights can we extract from the data?"

Analyzed sales trends across different time periods, product categories, and geographic regions. Identified top-performing products, customer segments, and product classes. Discovered seasonal purchasing patterns by analyzing order dates and payment dates. Calculated customer lifetime value, average order values, and repeat purchase rates. Analyzed order status distribution, payment method preferences, and shipping patterns. Identified high-value customers and their purchasing behaviors. Calculated inventory metrics and product performance indicators.

Phase 4

Reporting & Documentation

"How do we make insights accessible and actionable?"

Created database views for commonly used metrics and complex calculations. Developed reusable SQL queries for regular business reporting including sales reports, customer analytics, and product performance dashboards. Documented all queries with comments explaining business logic and methodology. Created comprehensive SQL scripts covering database creation, data insertion, and analytical queries. Ensured code reusability and maintainability for ongoing business intelligence needs.
