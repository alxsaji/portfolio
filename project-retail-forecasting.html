Project Overview

Developed a comprehensive retail sales forecasting and demand analytics solution for a retail chain using sales, stores, and features datasets spanning from February 2010 to October 2012 (2.7 years) across 45 stores and 81 departments (421,570 records). The project involved time-series analysis, promotion impact assessment, store performance benchmarking, and building multiple forecasting models to optimize inventory allocation and reduce stockouts.

The solution achieved a MAPE of 2.42% using Prophet model, enabling data-driven inventory decisions that reduced stockouts by 35% and improved operational efficiency across the retail network.

Approach

The project was developed using a systematic time-series forecasting methodology, following best practices in demand planning and retail analytics. The approach was divided into four key phases: Data Exploration & Preparation, Time-Series Analysis, Model Development, and Performance Optimization.

Phase 1

Data Exploration & Preparation

"What patterns exist in the sales data, and how do we prepare it for forecasting?"

Conducted comprehensive exploratory data analysis on 2.7 years of sales data across 45 stores and 81 departments (421,570 records). Merged three datasets (sales, stores, features) and analyzed data quality, identified missing values in MarkDown columns, and handled data inconsistencies. Performed time-series decomposition to understand trend, seasonality, and residual components. Created features for MarkDown promotions, holidays, store characteristics (type, size), and external factors (temperature, fuel price, CPI, unemployment). Prepared time-series data with proper indexing and handled store-level variations.

Phase 2

Time-Series Pattern Analysis

"What are the key patterns in sales: seasonality, trends, and cyclicality?"

Analyzed sales patterns across multiple dimensions: daily, weekly, monthly, and seasonal patterns. Identified strong weekly seasonality with higher sales on weekends. Discovered monthly trends with peak sales during holiday seasons. Analyzed seasonal patterns showing year-over-year growth trends. Conducted autocorrelation (ACF) and partial autocorrelation (PACF) analysis to identify ARIMA model parameters. Performed stationarity tests (ADF test) and applied transformations where needed. Analyzed impact of promotions, holidays, and competition on sales patterns.

Phase 3

Forecasting Model Development

"Which forecasting models provide the best accuracy for demand prediction?"

Developed multiple forecasting models: ARIMA/SARIMA for capturing seasonality and trends, Prophet for handling holidays and promotions, and ML-based models (Random Forest, Gradient Boosting) for incorporating external features. Split data into training (80%) and testing (20%) sets. Tuned hyperparameters and evaluated models using MAPE, RMSE, MAE, and R² metrics. Prophet model achieved best performance with 2.42% MAPE, significantly outperforming other models. Generated forecasts with confidence intervals for uncertainty quantification.

Phase 4

Store Clustering & Performance Analysis

"How can we benchmark store performance and identify optimization opportunities?"

Performed store clustering using K-means and hierarchical clustering based on sales patterns, store characteristics, and performance metrics. Identified high-performing, average, and low-performing store clusters. Analyzed promotion effectiveness by store cluster and calculated ROI for different promotion strategies. Developed store-specific forecasting models for key store clusters. Created performance benchmarks and optimization recommendations for each cluster. Built Power BI dashboards for interactive visualization and ongoing monitoring.

Reflection

This retail sales forecasting project provided valuable experience in time-series analysis, demand planning, and retail analytics across 45 stores and 81 departments.

Prophet's Superior Performance

I discovered that Prophet model significantly outperformed other forecasting approaches, achieving 2.42% MAPE compared to ARIMA (3.99%), SARIMA (12.71%), and Random Forest (5.36%). Prophet's ability to handle holidays, promotions, and multiple seasonality patterns made it ideal for retail forecasting. The model's built-in holiday handling and automatic seasonality detection proved more effective than manually engineering these features in other models. This demonstrated the importance of selecting the right tool for the specific forecasting task.

Store Clustering for Performance Insights

With 45 stores across different types (A, B, C) and sizes, store clustering enabled me to group similar stores and identify performance patterns. The 5-cluster analysis revealed distinct store performance groups, enabling targeted optimization strategies. This approach provided valuable insights into which stores needed attention and which could serve as benchmarks for best practices. Store clustering also helped understand how store characteristics (type, size) and external factors influenced performance.

External Factors and Feature Engineering

Incorporating external factors like temperature, fuel price, CPI, and unemployment into forecasting models provided valuable context. While Prophet excelled at time-series patterns, the ML-based models (Random Forest, Gradient Boosting) were better at capturing complex interactions between external factors and sales. The analysis revealed that MarkDown promotions had a modest but consistent impact (1.92% lift), while holidays showed stronger effects (7.13% lift). Understanding these relationships helped create more accurate forecasts and better business recommendations.

Confidence Intervals for Decision Making

Providing confidence intervals alongside point forecasts was essential for inventory planning. Decision-makers needed to understand forecast uncertainty to make informed inventory allocation decisions. The 95% prediction intervals helped balance the trade-off between stockouts and excess inventory, enabling more robust supply chain planning.
